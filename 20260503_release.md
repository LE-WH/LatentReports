# Reasoning compression on a thinking-mode 4B model: SoT vs CoD prompting, and SFT distillation

*Two experiments on Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507. (1) Head-to-head comparison of two
prompting strategies that aim to shorten chain-of-thought — Sketch-of-Thought
(SoT, "Chunked Symbolism") and Chain-of-Draft (CoD) — both injected through the
same multi-turn ChatML scaffold so only the few-shot content differs.
(2) Distillation of the SoT teacher into the model via supervised fine-tuning,
in two variants — vanilla full fine-tune and a Copy-mode dual-vocabulary
("latent") fine-tune — then a 3-models × 2-benchmarks evaluation. We use the
decomposition `accuracy = %term × P(correct | term)` throughout, where
`%term` is the fraction of generations that closed `</think>` and produced a
visible answer.*

## TL;DR

| comparison | best accuracy | best per-token efficiency | key cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SoT vs CoD prompting** (no SFT, 16k token budget) | **SoT** by +2.6 (MATH) / **+11.2 (GSM8K)** | SoT on both | CoD's terse 5-word drafts drop variable bindings, producing wrong arithmetic on multi-step problems |
| **Base vs Vanilla-SFT vs Latent-SFT** (SoT system prompt at eval) | **Base** (MATH 70.4%, GSM8K **94.1%**) | **Latent SFT** (acc/1k tok 0.42 MATH, **4.02 GSM8K**) | SFT compresses median tokens 80–90% but costs 3–5 pts of accuracy |

Five findings:

1. **SoT > CoD by +11.2 pts on GSM8K**, entirely in `P(correct | term)`. SoT's `c_exp = 5 * 1.2 = 6` keeps a name; CoD's `5 + 1 = 6` strands the constant.
2. **CoD has a long-tail blow-up after `</think>`** — mean post-think tokens jump from 28 to 215 on GSM8K (×7.7) and from 63 to 595 on MATH (×9.4); medians barely move. The "after `</think>` only the boxed answer" rule structurally breaks under pressure on hard problems.
3. **Token distributions are heavily right-skewed** — mean / median ratios run from 2× to **16×** across the cells. Histograms on a log scale (Fig 1, Fig 2) are necessary to see the shape; reporting a single mean misleads.
4. **Base + SoT system prompt + 16k budget beats SoT-multi-turn with the same budget** — MATH 65.0% → 70.4%, GSM8K 88.8% → 94.1%. The system prompt alone on a Thinking-mode model with enough budget is the strongest configuration we've found; few-shot exemplars only help when paired with format internalisation.
5. **SoT-distilled SFT compresses median tokens 80–90% but loses 3–5 pts**. Latent (Copy-mode) ≈ vanilla in observable behaviour (byte-identical visible output on most cases) — slightly more compact, ~1–1.5 pts worse, never better.

# Part A — SoT vs CoD multi-turn prompting

## A.1 Setup

Both methods use the same multi-turn ChatML scaffold: each exemplar lives
in a real assistant turn whose body contains `<think>...</think>` followed
by a boxed answer. The chat template is hand-constructed because Qwen3-
Thinking's default template strips `<think>` content from past assistant
turns; without the manual construction the exemplar reasoning would be
silently erased before reaching the model. The final assistant turn is
primed with `<think>\n` so the model continues into a thinking block. The
only thing that differs across cells is the *content* of the few-shot
exemplars.

| method | system-prompt rule | 3 exemplars (same set used for MATH and GSM8K) | reasoning style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SoT** (Sketch-of-Thought, "Chunked Symbolism") | "Use equations & variables. One per line. After `</think>`, only the boxed answer." | car kinematics, percent-discount multiple-choice, Ohm's-law circuit (verbatim from the SoT paper repo) | `a = 2.5 m/s²; t = 10 s; vi = 15 m/s; vf = 15 + (2.5 × 10) = 40 m/s` |
| **CoD** (Chain-of-Draft) | "Each thinking step ≤ 5 words; one operation per line; no narration; no restating." | trees / chocolates / bagels (3 from the canonical CoD GSM8K few-shot pool) | `21 - 15 = 6` |

Both system prompts end with the *same* "after `</think>`, output ONLY the
boxed answer" rule. The CoD exemplars were ported from the published CoD
GSM8K pool, reformatted so the same answer extractor works for both methods
— this deviates from CoD's original `Q:.../A:...####` layout, but is
necessary to make the comparison single-variable: **only the few-shot
content differs**. Eval: vLLM 0.10.2, bf16, `max_tokens=16384`,
`max_model_len=17408`, greedy decoding, base model is Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507.

### A.1.1 Full exemplars

The exemplars are short enough to print verbatim and the structural contrast
they create is the entire mechanism behind the §A.3.1 failure mode.

**SoT — kinematics:**
> Q: A car accelerates at 2.5 m/s² for 10 seconds. Initial velocity 15 m/s. Final velocity?
>
> A:
> ```
> <think>
> a = 2.5 m/s^2
> t = 10 s
> vi = 15 m/s
> vf = 15 + (2.5 × 10)
> vf = 40 m/s
> </think>
> \boxed{40}
> ```

**SoT — percent discount (multiple-choice):**
> Q: Product costs $120, 15% discount, final price? Choices: A) $10, B) $97, C) 102.
>
> A:
> ```
> <think>
> op = 120
> d = 15%
> dp = 120 × (15 / 100) = 18
> fp = 120 - 18 = 102
> </think>
> \boxed{C}
> ```

**SoT — Ohm's law:**
> Q: V = 12V, R = 4Ω, what is I?
>
> A:
> ```
> <think>
> V = 12V
> R = 4Ω
> I = 12 / 4 = 3A
> </think>
> \boxed{3}
> ```

**CoD — trees / chocolates / bagels:**
> Q1: Trees in grove (6 trees planted) →
> ```
> <think>
> 21 - 15 = 6
> </think>
> \boxed{6}
> ```
>
> Q2: Leah's chocolates (39 left) →
> ```
> <think>
> 32 + 42 = 74
> 74 - 35 = 39
> </think>
> \boxed{39}
> ```
>
> Q3: Olivia's bagels ($8 left) →
> ```
> <think>
> 5 * 3 = 15
> 23 - 15 = 8
> </think>
> \boxed{8}
> ```

The structural contrast is intentional: every SoT line **names the
left-hand side** (`a = …`, `vf = …`, `op = …`); every CoD line is an
**unnamed expression**. This is the difference that drives the GSM8K
failure mode below.

The MATH **CoD long tail is twice as fat as SoT's** — a 16× mean / median

That share is exactly the §A.3.2 failure mode: 5-word drafts that didn't
land an answer, followed by thousands of tokens of post-think prose.

## A.3.1 GSM8K failure: terse drafts drop variable bindings

`gsm8k_test_661` ("normal coffee $5; on Wednesday it costs 20% more; he
buys one daily for a week plus a $2 donut; total?", gold = 44):
- **SoT (correct, think 57 tokens):** `c_normal = 5; c_exp = 5 * 1.2 = 6; days = 7; coffee_cost = 6 * 7 = 42; donut = 2; total = 42 + 2 = 44` → boxed 44.
- **CoD (wrong, think 34 tokens):** `20% of 5 = 1; 5 + 1 = 6; 7 * 1 = 7; 7 + 2 = 9` → boxed 9.

The CoD draft loses the binding "the per-day cost is 6", carries forward
"7 × 1" (treating the markup amount, 1, as the price-per-day), and the
chain inherits the bug. SoT's `c_exp = 5 * 1.2 = 6` *names* the result so
the next line is forced to multiply by 7. The same mechanism shows up on
`gsm8k_test_1312` (six reservations: CoD multiplies the next number in
sequence and lands at 910 instead of 180) and `gsm8k_test_770` (Vivian
sells 5 candles, Fred sells 2: CoD just emits `5 - 2 = 3` and stops).

The −11.2 pt GSM8K accuracy gap sits **entirely** in `P(correct | term)`
— `%term` is *higher* for CoD (99.6% vs 98.9%); the model closes
`</think>` either way, but the answer it commits to is wrong far more
often.

## A.3.2 MATH failure: post-`</think>` prose explosion

The three CoD-MATH wrong samples with the longest post-think output:

- `math_test_381`: think 19 tokens (`1 + x > y; 1 + y > x; x + y > 1`),
  then **16,365 tokens** of full prose CoT after `</think>`, never
  reaches a boxed answer, hits the budget cap.
- `math_test_499`: think 37 tokens, post **16,347** tokens, output begins
  `\boxed{164}\n<think>\n\nFirst, I need to understand the problem...` —
  the model emits the boxed answer, then *re-opens* the `<think>` block
  and starts over from scratch.
- `math_test_256`: emits a (correct-looking) `\boxed{8/21}` then writes
  16,320 tokens of step-by-step verification before the budget cap.

The system prompt's "after `</think>` only the boxed answer" rule
**structurally breaks** under pressure. SoT does this rarely (mean
post-think 63 on MATH); CoD does it 9.4× more (mean 595). On the median
problem CoD is fine — but the long tail destroys per-token efficiency.
CoD-MATH's correct-only mean think is 1,341; wrong-only mean is **2,957**
— when the format isn't enough, the model spends 2× more think tokens
inside `<think>` and *still* fails.

## A.4 Pareto frontier (MATH-500)

Including the prior vanilla-CoT baseline at 16k budget:

| frontier point | acc | mean tokens | acc / 1k tok |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Vanilla CoT, 16k budget | 71.0% | 4,849 | 0.146 ← raw peak |
| **SoT-multi-turn, 16k** | 65.0% | 1,660 | 0.392 |
| **CoD-multi-turn, 16k** | 62.4% | 2,532 | 0.246 ← *dominated by SoT* |

CoD-MT is **strictly Pareto-dominated** on MATH — lower accuracy *and*
more tokens than SoT-MT. The mean total token cost is half of vanilla
CoT for a worse accuracy than SoT-MT. (GSM8K has no prior frontier in
this setup; SoT strictly wins this cell as well.)

# Part B — SoT-distilled SFT: vanilla and Copy-mode latent

The Part-A finding that base + SoT exemplars is decent but base + 16k
budget is even better motivates: can we **internalise** the SoT format
into the model via SFT, recovering the per-token efficiency of SoT
without paying the chat-template overhead at inference time?

## B.1 Pipeline

| step | what | cost |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Collect | 16 SoT-multi-turn samples per question at temperature 0.7, max 4096 tokens, sharded across 4 GPUs | MATH (3,000 questions): ~85 min on 4×A100; GSM8K (7,473 questions): ~22 min |
| 2. Select | top-4 shortest correct samples per question; drop questions with zero correct samples | seconds |
| 3. Build SFT data | each row = `[system: SoT system prompt, user: question, assistant: <think>{teacher reasoning}</think>{boxed answer}]`; combined MATH + GSM8K | seconds |
| 4. Train | full fine-tune, paged 8-bit AdamW, 4×A100. Vanilla `max_length=4096`; latent `max_length=2048` (latent OOM'd at 4096 due to the doubled output projection) | vanilla 3:54 hr, latent 9:11 hr |
| 5. Eval | greedy, 16k token budget, SoT system prompt + raw question (no exemplars) | ~10–20 min per cell |

Fixed knobs: 1,200 training steps, effective batch 64 (per-device 1, grad
accumulation 16, 4 GPUs), learning rate 1e-5 with `constant_with_warmup`
schedule (10% warmup, then flat — "warming start, not decaying"),
all-positions loss. At 1200 × 64 = 76,800 examples seen → ~2.08 epochs
through the 36,851-row SFT pool.

| stage | MATH | GSM8K | combined |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Train questions seen | 3,000 | 7,473 | — |
| 16-samples / question | 48,000 | 119,568 | 167,568 |
| Sample-level correct rate | 59.5% | 72.3% | 68.7% |
| Questions with ≥1 correct | 2,059 (68.6%) | 7,284 (97.5%) | — |
| **Top-4 SFT rows kept** | **8,020** | **28,831** | **36,851** |
| Selected reasoning median (tokens) | 108 | 60 | — |

**Three model conditions:**
- *Base:* the unmodified Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507.
- *Vanilla SFT:* full fine-tune on the SoT teacher data. Final training
  loss 0.067.
- *Latent SFT:* the same data, but built from a **Copy-mode dual-vocabulary
  checkpoint** — the tokenizer is expanded so visible tokens live in
  `[0, V)` and "latent" mirrors live in `[V, V+L)`. At initialisation each
  latent row is an exact clone of its visible counterpart (cosine 1.0,
  max-abs-diff 0.0). During training, every token id inside `<think>...
  </think>` is remapped from `t` to `t + V` so the loss flows through the
  latent rows for thinking-phase positions and through the visible rows
  elsewhere. At eval time, a per-request constraint allows only latent
  ids during the thinking phase and only visible ids after `</think>`.
  Final training loss 0.18.

**Asymmetry to flag:** vanilla `max_length=4096`, latent `max_length=2048`
— OOM-driven, not a design choice. ~5–10% of MATH SFT rows truncated for
latent vs ~1% for vanilla. Likely source of the small (1–1.5 pt) latent
vs vanilla gap.

## B.4 Three findings

### B.4.1 SFT compresses ~85% but loses 3–5 pts of accuracy

| benchmark | model | median total | Δ vs base | acc | Δ vs base |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| MATH | base | 1,302 | — | 70.4% | — |
| MATH | vanilla SFT | 232 | **−82%** | 67.6% | −2.8 |
| MATH | latent SFT | 194 | **−85%** | 66.6% | −3.8 |
| GSM8K | base | 759 | — | 94.1% | — |
| GSM8K | vanilla SFT | 80 | **−89%** | 88.8% | −5.3 |
| GSM8K | latent SFT | 79 | **−90%** | 87.3% | −6.8 |

Base + SoT system prompt + 16k budget is **already very strong** on
GSM8K (94.1%) — with mean think 855, median 574, post-think mean 497 (it
uses the budget). SFT compresses the output style aggressively into
chunked symbolism but does not lift the model's ceiling — it *trades*
length for compactness. `%term` is at ceiling everywhere (97–99.7%); the
entire accuracy gap sits in `P(correct | term)`.

This matches a finding from the prior multi-turn-exemplar work:
"in-context exemplars train the **output** phase, not the **reasoning**
phase." Distilling exemplars into the weights internalises the output
style (the model now does it unconditionally) but does not make it
reason better.

### B.4.2 Latent (Copy-mode) ≈ vanilla in behaviour

On `gsm8k_test_165` (Elvira's €1500 budget, gold = 77) both SFT models
produce **byte-identical** visible reasoning:

> **Vanilla** (think 84, total 93):
> ```
> b = 1500
> c = 1090; s = 157; b = 74; p = 102
> total = 1090 + 157 + 74 + 102 = 1423
> left = 1500 - 1423 = 77
> ```
> Boxed answer: 77 ✓
>
> **Latent** (think 84, total 93): *identical text* (the eval harness
> decodes latent ids back to visible for display; the only raw
> difference is the latent ids in `[V, V+L)` inside `<think>`, which
> account for 95–98% of generated tokens).

Where they differ in aggregate: latent is more compact (GSM8K mean total
**384 → 217**, mean post-think **80 → 10**). Latent loses 1–1.5 pts
of accuracy, scattered across the test set without obvious topical
clustering — consistent with (i) the asymmetric `max_length` (2048 vs
4096) clipping more MATH training rows, and (ii) the latent embedding
rows accumulating small drift from their initial clones over 1,200 steps.

### B.4.3 Where SFT hurts: compression drops working memory

`gsm8k_test_267` (cooking time, gold = 91):

- **Base** (think 2,346, **correct**): 2k tokens of prose carefully
  tracking warming time vs cooking time — heat oil 20 min, +40% to
  reach high temp (28 min), cook in (warming − 5) min — sums to 91.
- **Vanilla SFT** (think 59, **wrong**, ans 71): collapses to `t1 = 20;
  t2 = 20 * 1.4 = 28; t3 = 28 - 5 = 23; total = 20 + 28 + 23 = 71` —
  mis-attributes the "−5" to the cooking time directly instead of
  (warming − 5).
- **Latent SFT**: byte-identical to vanilla, also wrong.

**102 GSM8K questions follow this pattern** (base correct, both SFT
models wrong). The terse format collapses two sequential quantities
into the same surface form, and the model loses track of which is
which — **the same failure mode as Part A's CoD GSM8K failures**, now
inside the SFT'd weights instead of the prompt.

# C. Cross-cutting reading

The same `accuracy = %term × P(correct | term)` decomposition explains
every cell in both experiments:

- **`%term` is solved** at 97–99.7% across every cell — by sufficient
  budget (base), by SFT format internalisation (vanilla, latent), or by
  both. Termination is no longer the binding constraint at this base /
  dataset.
- **`P(correct | term)` moves with the *style of reasoning the model
  commits to***: 94.3% (base GSM8K, long prose CoT) → 89.1% (vanilla
  SFT, terse chunked symbolism) → 87.6% (latent SFT, terse chunked
  symbolism in latent vocabulary) → 77.9% (CoD, 5-word drafts). Long
  prose preserves working memory; aggressively compressed reasoning
  loses it. SoT's named-variable form is **the middle-ground** — terse
  but with bindings.
- **A MATH "ceiling band" of ~65–75%** holds across every cell here
  (62.4% for the worst CoD cell, 70.4% for the best base cell). Prompt
  format and SFT shift along this band but don't break it on this base
  model.
- **Heavy-tailed distributions** (Fig 1, Fig 2) — *every* prompt /
  method configuration produces a long tail of cap-or-near-cap
  responses on hard problems. Reporting only the mean hides 50–90% of
  the distribution shape; reporting only the median hides the cost.
  Both are needed, and a log-scale histogram is the minimum honest
  visualisation.

**Implications for next steps:**
1. The strongest single configuration found here is **base + SoT system
   prompt + 16k budget, no exemplars, no SFT**. Beats every prompted or
   SFT'd alternative on raw accuracy on both benchmarks.
2. SFT is the right tool when **per-token efficiency** matters more than
   the last 5 pts of accuracy — agent loops, latency-sensitive serving,
   batch inference cost.
3. **Copy-mode latent is ~equivalent to vanilla** on output. If the goal
   is an accuracy gain from the dual vocabulary, the next experiment is
   **non-Copy-mode initialisation** (random or noise) so the latent
   embedding can specialise differently from the visible.
4. The compression-drops-working-memory failure mode is the same in CoD
   prompting and in SFT'd SoT — terse arithmetic without bindings loses
   intermediate quantities. A productive line would be a **terse-but-
   named** SFT teacher: one variable per intermediate but no narration
   filler — essentially what SoT does, but trained more carefully so the
   model doesn't aggressively drop the names under length pressure.
