# Latent reasoning — final consolidated report (20260427)

*Synthesis of `20260427.md`, `20260427_sot_plan.md`, `20260427_2.md`, `20260427_merged.md`, `20260427_short.md`. Base model: `Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507`. Benchmark: MATH-500. Decomposition `accuracy = %term × P(correct | term)` (introduced in `20260420.md §4`) is the lens throughout — `%term` = fraction of generations that closed `</think>` and produced visible answer text.*

Four findings, in the order they happened, plus an implementation-verification note:

1. **Scaling SFT batch×epochs 4× does not fix low-ratio termination** — gains at middle ratios, no change at the bottom.
2. **Filtering compressed CoTs by `logprob_boxed` lifts middle ratios by +5 to +7 pts** — pure termination-pump; conditional accuracy unchanged.
3. **SoT prompting on the un-fine-tuned base** — looked like +16.8 pts at a 4k budget; controls collapse the gap to ~0–1 pt at matched 16k. What survives is per-token efficiency (~2.2× at 4k, ~1.8× at 16k).
4. **Putting `<think>` blocks *inside* SoT few-shot exemplars (multi-turn ChatML)** — drops median total tokens 1,573 → **208** and gives the best per-token efficiency on the frontier (0.392 acc/1k tok), at the cost of −5.4 pts vs SoT-on @ 16k.
5. **Verification:** the multi-turn `<think>`-incorporated inference is fully implemented and the 500-sample run produced the artefacts on disk.

P(correct | term) sits in a tight 73–77% band across every base / SFT / prompt configuration tested. **That's the model's MATH ceiling at this size; everything we win is termination, not reasoning.**

## 1. Scaling batch × epochs — does "more training" fix low-ratio termination?

Setup: same multirate TokenSkip SFT pipeline, only knob changed is `gradient_accumulation_steps: 8 → 32` (1 × 32 × 4 GPUs = global batch **128**), `max_steps=1200` fixed. Total samples seen 38.4k → 153.6k, i.e. 0.87 → ~3.5 epochs through the ~44k-row SFT set. Hypothesis: low-ratio failures at batch=32 were under-exposure to the `</think>` terminator token.

| Ratio | batch=32, 0.87 epochs | batch=128, ~3.5 epochs | Δ |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1.0 | 55.4% | **55.8%** | +0.4 |
| **0.7** | 63.4% | **65.8%** | **+2.4** |
| 0.5 | 45.0% | **48.2%** | +3.2 |
| 0.3 | 24.6% | **27.4%** | +2.8 |
| 0.1 | 9.0% | **9.2%** | +0.2 |

Termination breakdown:

| Ratio | Terminated (old → new) | P(correct \| term) | P(correct \| run-on) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1.0 | 344 → 347 (69% → 69%) | 77.9% → 77.2% | 5.8% → 7.2% |
| 0.7 | 423 → 431 (85% → 86%) | 74.5% → 75.6% | 2.6% → 4.3% |
| 0.5 | 298 → 307 (60% → 61%) | 74.5% → 76.2% | 1.5% → 3.6% |
| 0.3 | 156 → 160 (31% → 32%) | 69.9% → 74.4% | 4.1% → 5.3% |
| 0.1 | 42 → 54 (8% → 11%) | 64.3% → **50.0%** | 3.9% → 4.3% |

**Comment.** ratio=0.7 gains +2.4 to a new best of 65.8%; middle ratios pick up 2–3 pts. But termination at ratio=0.1 only moves 8% → 11%, and conditional accuracy at r=0.1 *regresses* from 64.3% to 50.0% — extra exposure to the thin, noisy 0.1 distribution teaches the model a worse way to terminate. **More training is not the fix at the aggressive end** — the terminator signal is too thin per row for standard next-token CE to learn no matter the epoch count. Motivates §2 (filter the training data instead).

## 2. Selecting better training data — quality filter on compressed CoTs

For each `(question, compressed_reasoning, ratio)` we run a single base-model forward pass on the SFT chat and sum realised-token logprobs over three character spans:

| score | range | typical token count |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `logprob_answer` | answer characters only (e.g. `2`, `25\pi`) | 1–22 (median 1) |
| `logprob_boxed` | `\boxed{ANSWER}` (open + answer + close) | 4–25 (median 5) |
| `logprob_post_cot` | `</think>` through end of `$\boxed{ANSWER}$` | 12–33 |

Implementation: bf16 realised-token gather + chunked fp32 `logsumexp` along time (256-token chunks) to avoid materialising a fp32 `(B, T, V)` tensor (V≈152K). Batch 8 fits on 40 GB H100; ~30 records/s/GPU; full ratio in ~7 min on a 4-way shard.

### 2.1 `logprob_answer` is mostly dead at non-aggressive ratios

Within-question score spreads (max − min, in nats) over the 5,048 questions with ≥2 surviving CoTs:

| ratio | lp_answer flat% | lp_boxed flat% | lp_post_cot flat% | lp_post_cot mean spread |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0.1 | **19.9%** | 0.0% | 0.0% | 5.54 |
| 0.3 | 75.1% | 0.0% | 0.0% | 2.60 |
| 0.5 | 89.3% | 0.0% | 0.0% | 2.63 |
| 0.7 | 91.9% | 0.0% | 0.0% | 4.61 |

`flat%` = fraction of multi-CoT questions where every CoT scores within 0.001 nats — i.e. the metric provides no ranking signal. **`logprob_answer` is flat in 89–92% of questions at ratios ≥0.5** because once `\boxed{` is in context the answer characters are over-determined. It only carries signal at ratio=0.1, where the CoT is lossy enough to break answer prediction. `logprob_boxed` and `logprob_post_cot` provide ranking signal everywhere.

### 2.2 Filter agreement — the metrics are not redundant

Jaccard of top-25%-per-question kept-sets across metrics:

| ratio | J(ans, post_cot) | J(boxed, post_cot) | J(ans, boxed) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0.1 | 0.309 | 0.309 | 0.353 |
| 0.5 | 0.178 | 0.227 | 0.263 |
| 0.7 | 0.166 | 0.235 | 0.244 |

### 2.3 Qualitative example — the filter selects CoTs that "actually deliver"

`math_train_3424` (gold = `100π`, 8 CoTs, `logprob_post_cot` spread = 22.4 nats):

- **Best (lp = −16.7)** reaches the substantive step — *"(x²+12x)+(y²+16y)=0 → half of 12 is 6, square 6²=36, add/subtract 36..."*
- **Worst (lp = −39.0)** re-states setup three times and runs out of budget — *"...equation circles equation find radius compute area rewrite equation standard form..."*

So the metric does what was asked of it: it discriminates "compressed CoT that actually delivers the answer" from "compressed CoT that runs out of room before getting to the answer."

### 2.4 SFT + eval on top-25%-per-question filtered by `logprob_boxed`

Same recipe as §1 batch=128 baseline. Filtered SFT row count: **16,756** (vs ~44k baseline) → **9.17 epochs** at 1200 steps. Best checkpoint by `eval_loss` was step 900 (loaded via `load_best_model_at_end`; step 1200 was measurably worse).

| ratio | baseline | filtered | **Δ** | %term base → filt | P(correct \| term) base → filt |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1.0 | 55.8% | 55.8% | +0.0 | 69% → 69% | 77.2% → 77.5% |
| 0.7 | 65.8% | 65.0% | −0.8 | 86% → 84% | 75.6% → 76.2% |
| **0.5** | 48.2% | **53.4%** | **+5.2** | 61% → **70%** | 76.2% → 74.7% |
| **0.3** | 27.4% | **34.4%** | **+7.0** | 32% → **41%** | 74.4% → 76.4% |
| 0.1 | 9.2% | 10.2% | +1.0 | 11% → 11% | 50.0% → 52.7% |

**Comment.** Lift is at r=0.3 (+7.0) and r=0.5 (+5.2), and the mechanism is pure termination — `%term` jumps +9 pts at both ratios while conditional accuracy stays flat (76±1%). Filtering for "the base model finds it easy to terminate after this CoT" attacks exactly the failure mode §1 left open. No-op at r=1.0 (data is uncompressed) and r=0.1 (every surviving CoT still too lossy to set up a clean `</think>`). −0.8 at r=0.7 is plausibly small-data noise on the already-saturated ratio (10 questions; conditional accuracy actually nudges up).

**Best SFT'd checkpoint to date: 65.8% at r=0.7 (baseline).** Filtering doesn't beat that on raw accuracy but it lifts the previously dominated middle ratios into the same band.

## 3. SoT — inference-time prompting on the un-fine-tuned base

Phase-0 prompt module + Phase-1 three-way eval on MATH-500. Base: Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507, **no SFT**. Three modes:

1. **Vanilla CoT** — chat template, ratio=1.0.
2. **SoT Chunked-Symbolism, thinking ON** — three exemplars inlined into a single user turn (the chat template silently strips `<think>...</think>` from past assistant turns on this model, so the paper's multi-turn exemplar layout degenerates to "Question → \boxed{Answer}" — see §4 for the structural fix).
3. **SoT Chunked-Symbolism, thinking SUPPRESSED** — append `\n</think>\n\n` to the prefilled `<think>\n` so the model starts in visible-output mode (`enable_thinking=False` does not work on the Thinking variant).

### 3.1 Headline (4k budget) — what looked like +16.8 pts

| Run | Accuracy | %term | avg think tok | avg total tok | gen time |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Vanilla CoT | 47.6% | 59.4% | 2,523 | 2,771 | 514 s |
| **SoT, thinking ON** | **64.4%** | **85.4%** | 1,788 | 1,964 | **385 s** |
| SoT, thinking SUPPRESSED | 62.6% | 100.0% | 0 | 2,248 | 464 s |

P(correct | term): vanilla 73.4%, SoT-on 74.0% — **identical**. The +16.8-pt gap is bought entirely by `%term` (59.4% → 85.4%). Same lever as §2's quality filter, delivered for free at inference. Note the vanilla 47.6% is the **un-fine-tuned base**; earlier reports' 55.8% number is the SFT'd checkpoint.

### 3.2 SoT-on changes the *post-thinking* output, not the thinking

Splitting captured SoT-on generations on `</think>`:

| question | total tok | think tok (~) | post tok (~) | think share |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| `math_test_363` (gold=2) | 493 | 323 | 39 | **89%** |
| `math_test_56` (gold=11) | 952 | 476 | 167 | **74%** |
| `math_test_297` (gold=103) | 1,216 | 683 | 39 | **95%** |

The post-think block is 39–167 tokens of clean Chunked-Symbolism; the `<think>` block is 8–18× larger and is essentially native chatty CoT. **`math_test_56`** ("sum of first N positive odd numbers is 121", gold 11) is the cleanest illustration:

*Inside `<think>` (~476 tokens, prose):*

```
Okay, let's see. The problem says the sum of the first N positive odd numbers is 121.
I need to find N. Hmm, I remember there's a formula [...] isn't it N squared? [...]
First 3: 1+3+5=9=3². Yeah, that seems right. So if the sum is 121, then N² = 121.
Therefore, N = sqrt(121) = 11.

[...verifies by re-deriving via Σ(2n−1) = 2·Σn − N = 2·66 − 11 = 121 ...]

Let me structure this in the required format. [...] In the first example, they wrote:
a = 2.5 m/s^2, t = 10 s, vi = 15 m/s, vf = 15 + (2.5 × 10), vf = 40 m/s. So they list
variables, then equations step by step. For this problem, the answer should be:
sum = N² ; 121 = N² ; N = √121 ; N = 11. But maybe they want it more concise. [...]
```

*After `</think>` (~167 tokens, terse CS):*

```
sum = N²
121 = N²
N = √121
N = 11
\boxed{11}
```

The think block does three things — solve, verify, and *meta-rehearse the SoT format by literally quoting the in-context exemplars at itself*. **In-context SoT exemplars train the output phase, not the reasoning phase.** Same pattern in `math_test_297` (post = 39 tokens, but think = 683 tokens of native verification of a modular-arithmetic walk).

Suppress-thinking is the only mode that produces end-to-end CS reasoning. `math_test_363` (gold `2`, full output, 105 tokens):

```
Let the number be x.
Equation: 3 + 1/x = 7/x
Solve for x:
1/x − 7/x = −3
−6/x = −3
6/x = 3
x = 6 / 3 = 2
\boxed{2}
```

Vanilla on the same problem: 1,029 tokens of internal reasoning + a markdown "Step 1 / Step 2 / Step 3 / Final Answer" write-up. The reasoning *arrives* in the first ~200 tokens of vanilla — the rest is over-verification.

### 3.3 Controls — the +16.8 was buying back run-on losses

| Run | Decoding | Budget | **Acc** | %term | %cap | P(c\|t) | mean tot |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Vanilla | greedy | 4k | 47.6% | 59.4% | 44.6% | 73.4% | 2,771 |
| Vanilla + sampling (T=0.6, top-p=.95, top-k=20, pres-pen=1.5) | sampled | 4k | 46.0% | 60.8% | 43.6% | 72.4% | 2,808 |
| **Vanilla** | greedy | **16k** | **71.0%** | 96.2% | 4.2% | 73.8% | 4,849 |
| SoT-on | greedy | 4k | 64.4% | 85.4% | 16.6% | 74.0% | 1,964 |
| SoT-suppress | greedy | 4k | 62.6% | 100.0% | 20.8% | 62.6% | 2,248 |
| **SoT-on** | greedy | **16k** | **70.4%** | 99.2% | 2.6% | 71.0% | **2,682** |
| SoT-suppress | greedy | 16k | 69.4% | 100.0% | 2.4% | 69.4% | 3,103 |

- **Token budget dominates.** 4k → 16k on vanilla CoT lifts +23.4 pts (47.6% → 71.0%) almost entirely via `%term` (59.4% → 96.2%). The 4k cap was strangling the unfine-tuned base.
- **Sampling does not help.** Control A is within run-to-run noise of greedy (−1.6 pts).
- **P(correct | term) ≈ 73–74% across vanilla-4k, vanilla-sampling, vanilla-16k, and SoT-on.** Four configs, one number — the model's MATH ceiling at this size.
- **At matched 16k budget, prompt format is a wash** — vanilla 71.0%, SoT-on 70.4%, SoT-suppress 69.4% (all within 1.6 pts).

### 3.4 What survives is per-token efficiency

Sorting all seven 4k+16k runs on (mean total tokens, accuracy):

| frontier point | mean total tok | acc | acc / 1k tok |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| **SoT-on 4k** | 1,964 | 64.4% | 0.328 |
| **SoT-on 16k** | 2,682 | 70.4% | 0.262 |
| **Vanilla 16k** | 4,849 | 71.0% | 0.146 |

Sampling, SoT-suppress (both budgets), and Vanilla-4k are dominated. **SoT-on @ 16k is the sweet spot before §4: ~99% of vanilla-16k's accuracy at ~55% of the tokens.** Median tokens are essentially budget-independent (SoT-on med_total = 1,562 at 4k vs 1,573 at 16k); the mean inflates with budget because the long tail does, not the typical case.

Calibration vs SFT'd checkpoints:

| setup | Accuracy | %term | P(correct \| term) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Base, no scaffold (vanilla 4k) | 47.6% | 59.4% | 73.4% |
| Base + vanilla 16k | 71.0% | 96.2% | 73.8% |
| Base + SoT prompt (4k) | 64.4% | 85.4% | 74.0% |
| Base + SoT prompt (16k) | 70.4% | 99.2% | 71.0% |
| Multirate SFT (no QF) — best, r=0.7 | 65.8% | 86% | 75.6% |
| Multirate SFT, qf0.25-boxed, r=0.5 | 53.4% | 70% | 74.7% |

Inference-time SoT @ 16k (70.4%) ≈ the strongest SFT'd model + budget. **SFT still adds reasoning quality** (P(correct | term) 75.6% vs 74.0%) — SoT closes the termination gap without closing the conditional-quality gap. Phase 2 (SFT-distill SoT) is the way to combine both.

## 4. Putting `<think>` blocks *inside* SoT few-shot exemplars (multi-turn ChatML)

§3.2 showed that in-context SoT exemplars train the output phase only — the model learns terse CS *after* `</think>`, not inside it. Two prior obstacles to fixing this:

1. The Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 chat template silently strips `<think>...</think>` content from past assistant turns, so a clean `[user, assistant_with_think, user, ...]` exemplar layout degenerates to `Question → \boxed{Answer}` with no reasoning visible.
2. The §3 workaround inlines exemplars as plain user-turn prose. In that slot the model reads exemplar `<think>` content as *post-reasoning style to imitate*, not as a structural cue for its own thinking.

**Implementation.** New `build_sot_multiturn_prompt` hand-constructs Qwen ChatML (`<|im_start|>{role}\n{content}<|im_end|>\n`) so each exemplar appears as a real `<|im_start|>assistant\n<think>\n[CS reasoning]\n</think>\n\boxed{...}<|im_end|>` turn — bypassing `apply_chat_template`'s stripping. System prompt strengthened with *"After `</think>`, output ONLY `\boxed{ANSWER}` — no equations, no summary, no prose. The `<think>` block itself must be terse Chunked-Symbolism."* Wired through `eval_raw.py` as `--sot-multiturn-exemplars`.

### 4.1 Result — 500-sample MATH-500 @ 16k budget, no SFT

| Run | **Acc** | mean total | mean think | **median total** | **median think** | acc / 1k tok |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Vanilla 16k | 71.0% | 4,849 | 4,318 | 3,092 | 2,550 | 0.146 |
| SoT-on 16k (inlined, §3) | 70.4% | 2,682 | 2,210 | 1,573 | 1,310 | 0.262 |
| SoT-on 4k (inlined, §3) | 64.4% | 1,964 | 1,788 | 1,562 | 1,341 | 0.328 |
| **SoT-on 16k (multiturn)** | **65.0%** | **1,660** | **1,597** | **208** | **198** | **0.392** |

**Behavior change confirmed.** The hypothesis lands at the structural level — multi-turn exemplars do reshape the `<think>` block. Examples on the same questions inspected in §3.2:

- `math_test_3` (divisors of 196). Baseline SoT-on `<think>` was hundreds of tokens of "Okay, let's see…" prose. Multi-turn `<think>`: `n = 196 \n 196 = 2² · 7² \n d(n) = (2+1)(2+1) = 9`. Three lines.
- `math_test_2` (sum of `f(-2) + f(-1) + f(0)` for `f(x) = (3x-2)/(x-2)`). Multi-turn `<think>`: pure CS — `f(x) = (3x-2)/(x-2)` then four numeric evaluations and the sum. No prose.
- `math_test_0` (rectangular → polar). Multi-turn `<think>`: `x=0 \n y=3 \n r=sqrt(0+9)=3 \n theta=arctan(y/x) but x=0 so theta=pi/2`. CS throughout.

Median total tokens collapse to **208** (was 1,573 baseline). The typical case is now: model emits a short CS think block, closes `</think>`, and outputs only `\boxed{}`.

### 4.2 Cost

Accuracy dropped 70.4% → 65.0% (−5.4 pts) on the same 500. Sample-level vs baseline: 317 both right, 8 multi-turn only, **35 baseline only**, 140 both wrong. The 35-vs-8 gap is the price of squelching native verification — the model arrives at an answer faster, but on hard problems the long verbose verification loop the baseline runs sometimes catches arithmetic slips that the terse CS pass misses.

### 4.3 Pareto frontier — multi-turn @ 16k strictly dominates SoT-on @ 4k

Updated non-dominated frontier on this benchmark:

| frontier point | acc | mean tokens | acc / 1k tok |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| **SoT-on 16k (multiturn)** | 65.0% | 1,660 | **0.392** ← best per-token |
| **SoT-on 16k (inlined)** | 70.4% | 2,682 | 0.262 |
| **Vanilla 16k** | 71.0% | 4,849 | 0.146 ← raw peak |

SoT-on @ 4k (the prior efficiency anchor at 0.328 acc/1k) is now dominated.

### 4.4 Implication for Phase 2

Multi-turn changes the teacher distribution available to SFT in a useful way: `correct-only` mean tokens for multi-turn is **1,083** vs baseline SoT-on's 1,969. Distilling from this distribution targets a much smaller `<think>` budget, and the Pareto-frontier framing for the SFT success criterion now has a stronger left endpoint to advance from. Next experiment: collect SoT-multiturn rollouts as the teacher data for Phase 2 instead of SoT-on (inlined), and see whether SFT can recover the −5.4 pt accuracy gap while keeping the −38% token reduction.

## 5. Implementation verification — multi-turn `<think>`-incorporated inference

Verified on disk after writing the §4 result:

- **Builder.** `sft/methods/sot/prompts.py:150` defines `build_sot_multiturn_prompt(question, *, paradigm="chunked_symbolism", suppress_thinking=False)`. It hand-constructs ChatML so each exemplar becomes a real `<|im_start|>assistant\n<think>\n…CS reasoning…\n</think>\n\boxed{…}<|im_end|>` turn (bypassing `apply_chat_template`'s stripping of `<think>` from past assistant turns), then primes the final assistant turn with `<|im_start|>assistant\n<think>\n` so the model continues from there.
- **Dispatch.** `sft/methods/sot/prompts.py:185` `build_sot_eval_prompt(...)` routes to `build_sot_multiturn_prompt` when `multiturn_exemplars=True`; otherwise falls back to the §3 inlined path through `apply_chat_template`.
- **CLI flag.** `sft/eval_raw.py:124` defines `--sot-multiturn-exemplars` (only meaningful with `--sot-prompt`); `:265` passes it into the prompt builder; `:390` and `:481` propagate it into the run name and W&B config.
- **Artefacts.** `results/sft/math/phase1_sot/eval/sot_thinking_on_multiturn_16k.{jsonl,log}` both exist on disk — the §4 numbers were actually produced, not paper-only.

## 6. Cross-cutting reading

The same `accuracy = %term × P(correct | term)` decomposition explains all four results:

- **§1** scaling batch×epochs leaves the `%term` floor at r=0.1 essentially intact (8% → 11%).
- **§2** filtering by `logprob_boxed` lifts `%term` by +9 pts at r=0.3 and r=0.5 with no change in P(correct | term) — pure termination-pump.
- **§3** SoT prompting lifts `%term` by +26 pts at the 4k budget with P(correct | term) unchanged; raising the budget to 16k makes both the prompt-only and SoT lifts collapse to within 1.6 pts.
- **§4** structurally relocating the SoT exemplar `<think>` blocks pushes median total tokens from 1,573 to 208 and gives the best per-token efficiency point known on this benchmark, with `%term` ≈ saturated and a small (−5.4 pt) accuracy cost from lost verification.

P(correct | term) sits in a tight 73–77% band across every configuration tested. Phase-2 SoT-SFT should target the (accuracy, mean-tokens) frontier defined by `(SoT-on 16k multiturn, 1660, 65.0%)` → `(SoT-on 16k inlined, 2682, 70.4%)` → `(Vanilla 16k, 4849, 71.0%)` and beat at least one of (a) 71% raw accuracy or (b) the per-token efficiency line connecting those points. The most promising direction: **distill the multi-turn teacher (1,083-token correct-only mean) and try to recover the −5.4 pt accuracy gap**.
